% ---- Abstract -----------------------------------------------
\begin{abstract}
We design and evaluate a fully automated tactical asset allocation (TAA) strategy
targeting corporate cash management, where the primary constraint is preserving
capital against severe drawdowns while achieving returns above inflation.
The strategy combines multi-period momentum scoring, minimum-correlation
portfolio selection, and a market-breadth crash filter to dynamically rotate
across a diversified universe of 15 exchange-traded funds (ETFs) on a monthly basis.

Backtested over 2008--2025 using a strict train/out-of-sample split
(2008--2019 / 2020--2025), the strategy achieves a compound annual growth rate (CAGR)
of 11.27\% with a maximum drawdown of $-13.87$\% over the full period,
satisfying the hard corporate constraint of $\text{MaxDD} < -15\%$.
Out-of-sample performance (CAGR 12.05\%) confirms in-sample results.
During the Global Financial Crisis (2008--2009), the strategy returns $+17.64\%$
while equities lose more than 33\%.
A European UCITS implementation achieves virtually identical performance
(CAGR 12.35\% net of commissions, 2019--2026), confirming cross-market applicability.

Our results contribute to the growing literature on systematic, rule-based TAA
by demonstrating that momentum-based rotation with an explicit crash detection
mechanism can deliver equity-like returns within corporate treasury risk constraints,
without recourse to leverage or derivatives.
\end{abstract}
