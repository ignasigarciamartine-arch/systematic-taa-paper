% ---- Abstract -----------------------------------------------
\begin{abstract}
We design and evaluate a fully automated tactical asset allocation (TAA) strategy
for corporate cash management, where the relevant objective is not unconditional
return maximisation but capital preservation subject to a hard drawdown constraint.
The strategy combines multi-period momentum scoring, minimum-correlation portfolio
selection, and a market-breadth crash filter to rotate monthly across a diversified
universe of 15 exchange-traded funds (ETFs), without leverage, derivatives, or
discretionary intervention.

Using a strict train/out-of-sample split over 2008--2025
(2008--2019 / 2020--2025), the strategy achieves a full-period compound annual
growth rate (CAGR) of 11.47\%, a Sharpe ratio of 0.974, and a maximum drawdown of
$-13.79$\%, satisfying the corporate constraint of $\text{MaxDD} > -15\%$.
Sharpe and Sortino ratios are computed from monthly excess returns over the
3-month U.S. Treasury Bill rate. Out-of-sample performance remains stable,
with a CAGR of 12.54\% and maximum drawdown of $-10.84\%$ over 2020--2025.
During the Global Financial Crisis
(2008--2009), the strategy returns $+17.64\%$ while the S\&P 500 loses
$-33.05\%$. A European UCITS implementation achieves comparable performance
(gross CAGR 12.71\% over March 2019--March 2026; 12.35\% net in the
transaction-cost window).

The results contribute to the literature on systematic, rule-based TAA by showing
that momentum-based rotation, when combined with an explicit breadth-based crash
filter, can deliver equity-like returns while respecting corporate treasury risk
constraints in the realised historical and out-of-sample sequence.
A stricter strategy-level bootstrap, however, shows that this result is not
robust across arbitrary resampled asset-return histories, materially narrowing
the scope of the performance claim.
\end{abstract}
