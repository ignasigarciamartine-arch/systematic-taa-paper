\subsection{Asset Universe and Data}

The investment universe consists of 15 risky ETFs covering six geographic
equity regions, real estate, commodities, and fixed income, plus one cash
proxy (Table~\ref{tab:universe}).
All prices are adjusted daily closing prices sourced from Refinitiv Eikon.
Cash returns and the risk-free rate used in performance ratios are computed from
the monthly 3-month U.S. Treasury Bill rate sourced from Refinitiv/Federal
Reserve data \citep{fred_dgs3mo}; the annual yield is converted to a monthly
return as $(1+y_t/100)^{1/12}-1$.
The sample spans January 2007 to December 2025; the year 2007 serves as
a warm-up period for the 12-month momentum lookback, so the effective
backtest starts in January 2008.

The backtest uses monthly rebalancing with a strict train/out-of-sample split:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Training period}: January 2008 -- December 2019 (142 months).
          All parameters are selected exclusively within this window.
    \item \textbf{Out-of-sample period}: January 2020 -- December 2025 (72 months).
          No parameter is adjusted after this split.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Multi-Period Momentum Score}

For each risky asset $i$ at month-end $t$, we compute the
13612U momentum score as the equal-weight average of four lookback returns:

\begin{equation}
M_{i,t} = \frac{1}{4}\left(R_{i,t}^{1} + R_{i,t}^{3} + R_{i,t}^{6} + R_{i,t}^{12}\right)
\label{eq:momentum}
\end{equation}

where $R_{i,t}^{k}$ is the $k$-month total return of asset $i$ ending at month $t$.
Equal weighting across four horizons reduces sensitivity to any single lookback
window, an approach motivated by the robustness evidence in \citet{moskowitz2012}.

Assets are ranked in descending order of $M_{i,t}$; the top $N = 7$ form the
candidate set. An absolute momentum threshold $\theta = 2\%$ is then applied:
only assets with $M_{i,t} \geq \theta$ pass into the eligible set $\mathcal{A}_t^+$.
The threshold implements the absolute momentum filter of \citet{antonacci2012},
ensuring the strategy does not hold assets in a negative trend.

\subsection{Minimum-Correlation Triplet Selection}

Portfolio construction depends on the size of $\mathcal{A}_t^+$:

\begin{equation}
\text{Portfolio}_t =
\begin{cases}
\text{Triplet}(\mathcal{A}_t^+) & \text{if } |\mathcal{A}_t^+| \geq 3 \\
\text{Defensive}_t              & \text{if } |\mathcal{A}_t^+| < 3
\end{cases}
\label{eq:selection}
\end{equation}

When $|\mathcal{A}_t^+| \geq 4$, the triplet is selected to minimise average
pairwise correlation across all $\binom{|\mathcal{A}_t^+|}{3}$ combinations:

\begin{equation}
\{a^*, b^*, c^*\} = \underset{\{a,b,c\} \subseteq \mathcal{A}_t^+}{\arg\min}
\;\frac{\rho_{ab} + \rho_{ac} + \rho_{bc}}{3}
\label{eq:triplet}
\end{equation}

where $\rho_{ij}$ is the Pearson correlation of daily returns of assets $i$ and $j$
computed over the calendar month $t$.
When $|\mathcal{A}_t^+| = 3$, all three eligible assets are held equally.
Each selected asset receives equal weight $w = 1/3$.
This selection rule operationalises the mean-variance insight of \citet{markowitz1952}
through a transparent, parameter-free criterion.

\subsection{Enhanced Defensive Allocation}

The defensive mode allocates to long-duration US Treasuries rather than
pure cash, exploiting the flight-to-quality phenomenon observed during
equity market stress:

\begin{equation}
\text{Defensive}_t =
\begin{cases}
\frac{1}{2}\,\text{TLT} + \frac{1}{2}\,\text{IEF} & \text{if } \max(M_{\text{TLT},t},\, M_{\text{IEF},t}) \geq 0 \\
\text{Cash} & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\label{eq:defensive}
\end{equation}

This ensures that even in defensive mode the strategy captures treasury rallies
that typically accompany equity sell-offs, rather than remaining idle in cash.
In implementation, the cash sleeve can be represented by short-duration Treasury
vehicles such as BIL; in the backtest it is credited with the 3-month T-bill
return to avoid cash-ETF distribution-adjustment artefacts.

\subsection{Market-Breadth Crash Filter}

Before executing the momentum signal, a crash filter is applied each month.
Let $\bar{r}_t$ denote the equal-weight average of the monthly returns of all
15 risky assets:

\begin{equation}
\bar{r}_t = \frac{1}{15} \sum_{i=1}^{15} r_{i,t}
\label{eq:breadth}
\end{equation}

If $\bar{r}_t < \tau$ where $\tau = -4\%$, a market crash is signalled and
the portfolio is immediately directed to the defensive allocation
(Equation~\ref{eq:defensive}), overriding the momentum signal.
This pre-empts momentum continuation under conditions of broad market stress,
addressing the momentum crash vulnerability documented by \citet{daniel2016}.
The threshold $\tau = -4\%$ was selected by in-sample optimisation
(Table~\ref{tab:threshold_sensitivity}).

\subsection{Performance Metrics}

Let $r_t$ denote the monthly portfolio return and $r_{f,t}$ the monthly
3-month U.S. Treasury Bill return. Risk-adjusted metrics are computed from
monthly excess returns $x_t = r_t-r_{f,t}$, not from a zero-rate convention.

\begin{align}
\text{CAGR} &= \left(\prod_{t=1}^{T}(1 + r_t)\right)^{12/T} - 1
\label{eq:cagr} \\[6pt]
\text{Sharpe} &= \frac{\sqrt{12}\,\bar{x}}{\sigma_x}
\label{eq:sharpe} \\[6pt]
\text{Sortino} &= \frac{\sqrt{12}\,\bar{x}}
{\sqrt{\frac{1}{T-1}\sum_{t=1}^{T}\min(x_t,0)^2}}
\label{eq:sortino} \\[6pt]
\text{Max DD} &= \min_{t} \frac{V_t - \max_{s \leq t} V_s}{\max_{s \leq t} V_s}
\label{eq:maxdd}
\end{align}

where $\bar{x}$ and $\sigma_x$ are the sample mean and standard deviation of
monthly excess returns, and $V_t$ is the portfolio value at month $t$.
Drawdowns are measured on end-of-month portfolio values.

\subsection{Benchmarks}

We compare the strategy against two passive benchmarks:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{60/40 portfolio}: 60\% SPY + 40\% IEF, rebalanced monthly.
    \item \textbf{Buy-and-hold equal weight (B\&H EW)}: equal allocation
          across all 15 risky ETFs, rebalanced monthly.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Robustness Checks}

We assess robustness through: (i) sensitivity of results to the crash threshold
$\tau \in \{-2.0\%, -2.5\%, -3.0\%, -3.5\%, -4.0\%, -4.5\%, -5.0\%\}$;
(ii) sensitivity to momentum specification (M6, M12, 13612U, 13612W);
(iii) a return-level circular block bootstrap with block length 6 months and
$N = 10{,}000$ simulations to assess sequencing risk in the realised strategy
return stream;
(iv) a strategy-level paired daily block bootstrap with block length 63 trading
days and $N = 10{,}000$ simulations, in which the asset return matrix is
resampled jointly and the momentum, crash filter, defensive switch, and triplet
selection are recomputed on each synthetic history; and
(v) a UCITS implementation using European-domiciled equivalent ETFs.
