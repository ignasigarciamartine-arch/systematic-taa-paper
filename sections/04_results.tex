\subsection{In-Sample Performance and Strategy Evolution}

Table~\ref{tab:evolution} shows how each component of the strategy contributes
incrementally to in-sample performance over 2008--2019.
The base strategy (momentum signal alone, with cash as the defensive asset)
achieves a CAGR of 8.75\% and a Sharpe ratio of 0.823.
Replacing the cash defensive allocation with an enhanced defensive mode
(TLT + IEF) raises CAGR to 10.75\% (+2.00 pp) and Sharpe to 0.933,
confirming the value of the flight-to-quality mechanism during stress periods.
Adding the market-breadth crash filter further improves CAGR to 10.94\% (+0.19 pp)
and Sharpe to 0.951, while leaving maximum drawdown controlled at $-13.79\%$.

\subsection{Crash Filter Threshold Sensitivity}

Table~\ref{tab:threshold_sensitivity} reports in-sample performance
across seven candidate values of the crash threshold $\tau$.
The CAGR profile is broadly flat between $\tau = -4.0\%$ and $\tau = -5.0\%$,
suggesting the selected value of $-4.0\%$ lies in a stable region of the
parameter space rather than at a sharp optimum.
Lower thresholds (closer to zero) over-trigger the filter and reduce
offensive exposure, while higher thresholds miss genuine crash signals.

Table~\ref{tab:oos} reports out-of-sample performance over 2020--2025,

maximum drawdown of $-10.84\%$, all comparable to or better than in-sample

\subsection{Crisis Period Analysis}

Table~\ref{tab:crisis} reports performance during three major stress episodes.
The crash filter is most valuable during the Global Financial Crisis:
with 6 triggers in 16 months, the strategy returns $+17.64\%$ versus
a loss of $-33.05\%$ for the S\&P 500.
During the COVID aftermath period, the strategy underperforms equities
in absolute terms ($+30.21\%$ vs $+41.04\%$), reflecting the opportunity
cost of defensive allocation during the sharp 2020--2021 rally.
The Tariff Shock of March--April 2025 shows zero drawdown for the strategy
versus $-6.67\%$ for equities, despite zero crash filter triggers,
illustrating that momentum positioning alone can sidestep short-term shocks
when the portfolio is already in defensive assets.

\subsection{Market Regime Analysis}

Table~\ref{tab:regimes} classifies each month into one of four regimes
based on SPY's trailing 6-month momentum and drawdown, following a
hierarchical scheme: CRISIS (6-month drawdown $< -10\%$),
BEAR (6-month momentum $< -5\%$), BULL ($\geq +5\%$), and SIDEWAYS (otherwise).

The strategy's defensive rate varies markedly across regimes: just 5.9\%
in BULL markets versus 66.7\% in CRISIS periods.
The strategy delivers its strongest relative outperformance in CRISIS
(+52.02 pp vs.\ 60/40) and SIDEWAYS (+3.46 pp) regimes.
In BEAR markets the strategy underperforms by $-43.77$ pp, but this estimate
is based on only nine months and should not be interpreted as stable.

\subsection{Monte Carlo Robustness}
\label{sec:montecarlo}

To assess sequencing risk in the realised return stream, we apply a stationary
block bootstrap \citep{politisromano1994} with data-driven block length
\citep{politiswhite2004} and $N = 10{,}000$ simulations on the realised
strategy monthly returns.
The stationary bootstrap draws blocks of geometrically distributed length,
ensuring each synthetic path is itself stationary and avoiding the wrap-around
artefact of the circular bootstrap.
The optimal block length, estimated from the strategy return series via the
method of \citet{politiswhite2004}, is $b = 3$ months, consistent with the
weak serial dependence (lag-1 autocorrelation: $-0.11$) of the monthly return
stream.
The strategy and 60/40 benchmark returns are resampled jointly with the same
block indices (paired bootstrap), so outperformance probabilities are computed
under identical sequence orderings.
This test does not re-optimise parameters or re-estimate signals; it evaluates
how sensitive CAGR, maximum drawdown, and relative performance are to the order
in which the historically realised returns arrive.

Table~\ref{tab:montecarlo} reports the bootstrap distribution.
The median CAGR of 11.45\% closely matches the historical 11.47\%; this
near-equality is a mathematical consequence of resampling from the fixed
realised return set and confirms correct implementation rather than
constituting evidence of strategy robustness.
The 10th-percentile CAGR is 8.24\%, remaining above the 7\% corporate
threshold; only 3.8\% of paths fall below that threshold
(P(CAGR $\geq 7\%$) $= 96.2\%$).
Drawdown outcomes are more path-dependent: the 10th-percentile simulated
maximum drawdown is $-19.43\%$, and the fraction of paths satisfying
MaxDD $> -15\%$ ranges from 65.6\% at block length $b = 3$ to 70.2\% at
$b = 6$, illustrating residual sensitivity to autocorrelation structure.
The historical drawdown constraint should therefore be interpreted as an
observed backtest property, not a guarantee across all return orderings.

The paired bootstrap provides additional evidence on relative performance.
In 98.2\% of simulated orderings S3.6 CAGR exceeds the 60/40 CAGR, and in
95.8\% the Sharpe ratio exceeds that of 60/40, indicating that the
outperformance is not an artefact of the specific sequence of returns realised
between 2008 and 2025.
The Ledoit--Wolf \citeyearpar{ledoitwolf2008} test of
$H_0\colon \text{SR}(\text{S3.6}) = \text{SR}(\text{60/40})$ yields a Sharpe
difference of $+0.44$ (annualised; 95\% CI: $[-0.03,\, +0.91]$; $p = 0.068$).
The interval marginally includes zero, so the null cannot be rejected at the
conventional 5\% level with 214 monthly observations; the evidence is
suggestive but not conclusive.

\subsection{UCITS Implementation}

To validate applicability for European-domiciled entities,
we replace the US ETFs with UCITS-compliant equivalents and
re-run the strategy over March 2019 -- March 2026 (83 months).
The UCITS strategy achieves a gross CAGR of 12.71\% over 83 months.
In the 82-transition transaction-cost window, the gross CAGR is 13.07\%
and the net CAGR is 12.35\% using Interactive Brokers fee schedules.
Commission drag is approximately 0.72 pp per annum, confirming that
transaction costs reduce but do not erase the strategy's performance profile.
